AI-assist posture ADR

extends Integrations & Syncing · cross-cuts 6 capabilities

Zero code. Fix the posture before any AI feature: (1) self-hosted small model default (Ollama/llama.cpp on the home server) vs a proprietary-API exception that must clear "significantly better"; (2) the LLM connector lives under Integrations; (3) standing guardrail — AI summarizes / extracts / answers over owned trip data, never generates the plan, never auto-commits, always human-confirmed.

High leverage Backed 3/3

The keystone that unblocks the rest. Six AI surfaces (digest narration, sentiment read, NL Q&A, expense categorization, recap, capture classification) touch six capabilities with no node, facet, or ADR — so model/privacy/placement get decided implicitly by whoever ships first, against Scott's open-source-first rule over PII-dense data (faces per ADR-0007, emails, finances). Keep it a one-pager — a decision, not a system to build.

Next Highest thesis leverage — mostly assembly of proven parts

3

Ideation When / Availability — the date-finding wedge

extends Ideation (When + Availability) → Itinerary → People & Membership

Own "when can WE actually go" — the moment that today happens entirely in Doodle/When2Meet before anyone opens Waypoint. (1) Relax NOT-NULL on trips.start_date/end_date behind a "forming" lifecycle state; (2) build Availability (member × date-range × yes/maybe/no) as a paint-the-grid heatmap; (3) winning window one-taps into dates. The poll IS the invite — answering it creates the Member.

High leverage Backed 3/3

The single moment upstream of the decision to use any tool — whoever owns date-finding owns the trip's birth, and it doubles as the viral invite. Schema blocker verified real (required:true in 0002_trips.js) — sequence the When/Availability half first. Hard line: ranks group-supplied candidates, must never auto-suggest destinations (brushes "not a discovery tool").

4

Email Digest → Re-engagement

extends Integrations & Syncing + Group Input, Ideation, People

Promote the committed one-way digest into the re-engagement spine: a weekly "what changed" email, LLM-narrated over the change-feed, carrying inline tokenized one-tap actions — vote, mark availability, RSVP — that write straight back via signed per-member token links (same trust primitive as join links), landing the member already-authed and already-counted.

High leverage Backed 3/3

Highest leverage-per-hour on the map. Attacks the charter's literal success definition ("the non-technical people actually use it") via the only sanctioned re-engagement lever — push is off the table. Resend + tokens already ship; this is composition. Two gates: structured writes only (reply-to-thread crosses "messaging beyond comments"); LLM narration waits on the AI ADR — ship the dumb cron'd diff first.

5

Per-item participation — a 5th input mechanism

extends Group Input + Money (split source) + Itinerary Item

A per-item participation mark distinct from assigned_to (which means "I'm responsible," not "I'm attending"): member × item → in/out, a one-tap "I'm in" on optional activity/meal items. Wire it as the default feeder for the Paid-Moment split so the split is right by construction, editable after.

High leverage Backed 3/3

Sharpest timing argument on the map. Code-verified: ExpenseForm prefills the split to the whole roster, so every non-whole-group dinner forces the payer to hand-deselect — the exact Splitwise friction Waypoint exists to kill (V1). Grill before #230 (Money Units) ships and bakes a whole-group-split assumption; the two-axis model (unit = settlement collapse; participation = split membership) must stay independent.

6

Decision provenance — persist the resolved choice + reasoning

extends Group Input / Ideation → Records & Archive

When a scenario/poll resolves, mint a small immutable Decision record: options weighed, the vote snapshot, the winning rationale, a link to what it produced. Distinct from a Comment (transient) and a Vote (a tally) — the outcome with its reasoning attached. Pairs with a "Consensus Lock" promote moment.

High leverage Backed 3/3

Extends the one thing the council says Waypoint already beats competitors at (the decision board) and is the structural antidote to the #1 group-text relapse: re-litigating closed decisions. Cheap — one row at promote-time, structured not messaging, nothing on the NOT-list touches it. Caveat: compounds with the Ideation wedge, so ship it alongside. Keep it a snapshot, not a discussion log.

7

Plan Health — deterministic structural intelligence

extends Itinerary + Trip Execution, Availability

Deterministic, explainable checks over the existing item graph + Availability + coords: anchor-time conflicts ("two things at 7pm"), day density ("Day 3 is a death march"), empty days, checkout-vs-departure mismatch, travel-time between coords, items outside a member's stated availability. Rule core first (no AI); LLM phrasing is optional polish. It surfaces; the owner decides.

High leverage Backed 3/3

Makes Itinerary the only group planner with an opinion — the difference between a shared document and a tool that tells you your plan is broken before trip day. Boundary is the whole ballgame: the NOT-list bars authoring the plan, not the plan being self-aware. Strong with the novice lens. Ship 2–3 high-signal checks, not a 15-rule linter nobody reads.

8

Live Money Pulse — group "are we overspending?"

extends Money (Glance / Budgeting) + Trip Execution

Extend the Money Glance from personal ("what I have left") to a group-pace lens in Trip Execution: spent-vs-budget burn rate, projected end-of-trip total, a quiet per-category hot-flag — unit-scoped per ADR-0015 with a group rollup. Derived only; single-currency; quiet/findable-not-nagging, never itemized to shame anyone.

Medium leverage Pass 0/3

Skeptics: on-thesis but a derived polish beat, not a multiplier. The collective "as a group, are we on pace?" question drives groups back to a shared Sheet — a place Splitwise structurally can't follow — and the data already exists. But it sharpens an already-present number rather than closing a seam. Build after the two-axis Money model settles.

9

Anticipation — name the front porch

extends new beat (Ideation→Itinerary) / pre-live Trip Execution

A mobile trip-home countdown banner mirroring the wrap-up banner at the opposite end of the timeline: days-to-go + what's-still-unbooked (the Readiness rollup) + who-said-they're-excited (Goals). Zero new data, pure composition. In-app only.

Medium leverage Pass 0/3

Skeptics: worth naming now, a small build later. The charter names four pillars but the spine is build/trip/record — anticipation, the loudest group-text moment, has no owned surface (only an lg-desktop-gated line invisible on the phone). ⚠ Guardrail is the whole game: hype without push, in-app only — the wrap-up PRD killed cron for a reason; don't smuggle push back in.

Settle-up confirmation receipt + "all settled" zero-state

extends Money / Settlement + Records (D5 wrap-up)

A settle-confirmation beat: a clear settled/unsettled state per Money Unit pair, an explicit "mark as settled" both sides see, and a satisfying "all settled up" zero-state on the Money glance. Scope to Money Units (debts wash within a unit per ADR-0015). Wire into the D5 sequence. Surface on open — no push reminders.

Medium leverage Pass 0/3

Skeptics: high-ROI closure beat, not a multiplier. Money transplants Splitwise's debt-simplification, but the settlements collection has no confirmation beat — task-path #31 ends at the calculation, not the acknowledgment. Splitwise's retention engine is the closure dopamine; transplant the closure, not the nagging. Cheap; reads cleanest after Money Units land.

11

Durable group / Crew — the cross-trip layer

extends People & Membership · net-new, currently unmapped

A user-level Crew above People & Membership: a named, persistent set of people a trip can be started from (pre-fills roster, seeds Money Units, warm onboarding) and contributed back to after. Start derived if needed — even "people you've traveled with before" from past members is a tracer bullet. The parent that recurring series, preference memory, and the living record all hang off.

High leverage Backed 3/3

The keystone the multi-trip flywheel hangs on — yet the single biggest scope-creep risk if mis-sequenced. The thesis is "the home for a GROUP trip," but every people-primitive is trip-scoped. Guardrail (D1 + scale invariant): an explicitly-decided shift of the product unit (trip → account), never a centralizing "mission control" hub; a Crew of one is just you. Do clone-carries-roster first as the cheap down-payment.

12

Members-only Trip Recap — the trip told back as a story

extends Records & Archive + Trip Memory, Money, Itinerary

A members-only post-trip Recap as a sibling to the Public Archive — not a new store, a read-composition: the day timeline threaded with reviewed Memories + the estimate-vs-actual money epilogue (#195) + goals-set-vs-hit. Public Archive = sanitized plan for the world; Trip Recap = felt story for the travelers. Optionally LLM-narrated for the private variant only.

High leverage Backed 3/3

Nobody owns the trip told back to the group as a story — the emotional payoff of the whole app. ⚠ Hard ADR-0007 constraint: a public recap draws only from done-items + budget, never memory thoughts (faces/PII); a memory-inclusive recap is private-keepsake-only. Bake the exclusion into the code path. Gated behind Trip Memory shipping first.

13

Replicable Archive — archive → stranger → new trip

extends Records & Archive + Ideation, Itinerary, Onboarding

Let a non-member viewing a public archive spin it into a fresh draft trip they own — structure, goals, and the curated "what we considered" recommendations, PII already stripped. The internal Clone is the member half; this archive→stranger→new-trip path is the viral half nobody owns. Frame "what we considered" as a Letterboxd-style list with one-tap "plan your own version."

Medium leverage Split 1/3

Skeptics split. One rates it high (the only organic acquisition loop besides the date-poll invite); two hold it medium — a growth bet whose payoff assumes archives get shared at volume (unproven), and it sits closest to the "not a discovery tool" line of any non-flagged item. Charter #39 names it exactly. Disposition both sides agree on: grill the boundary before building, and the internal Clone must be solid first.

14

clone-carries-roster — the cheap Crew down-payment

extends People & Membership (Clone) · review-fix elevated

Clone copies phases/days/items/checklists but carries zero co-travelers — so "start next year's trip from this one" (#36) reproduces the itinerary then makes the owner re-invite the same friends one email at a time. Add an opt-in "bring the same people" step that re-creates prior active members as placeholders with placeholder_email pre-filled, so accept/claim is one tap per person.

High leverage Split 1/3

Not in the opportunities array — surfaced as a review-fix and elevated. Only one skeptic scored it explicitly (high): "the highest-leverage-per-effort item adjacent to the Crew keystone." Code-verified the gap (clone hook recreates only the owner). A self-contained slice reusing shipped invite/claim machinery that delivers the Crew payoff before the full entity exists — and the cheapest way to test demand for Crew.

15

Durable member facts — diet / accessibility / budget ceiling

extends People & Membership (Identity) → Itinerary + Money

A lightweight per-member "trip profile": a small curated set of structured, optional, member-authored constraint fields. Consult-points: meal-item planning surfaces diet flags; a Money Unit budget override seeds from a member ceiling. A curated short list (like Memory's cap discipline), not a free-form profile. Co-traveler-visible only (ADR-0006).

Medium leverage Pass 0/3

Skeptics: real modeling gap, weak standalone — ride the Crew decision. A dietary restriction is a standing fact, not a preference you "vote" once and lose in a thread — yet nothing in the map carries member state that persists and gets consulted by other capabilities. But its payoff materializes at the durable-member level, so trip-scoped first risks throwaway work. Watch the scope edge: "won't-fly-redeye" is where a curated list quietly becomes a profile builder.

16

Calendar feed — webcal subscribe

extends Integrations & Syncing → Calendar sync

A per-trip webcal:// subscribed feed (each item keyed by id as UID so edits/deletes propagate with no duplicates) that puts the itinerary into Google/Apple Calendar once and keeps it correct forever, with a download-.ics fallback. A read-projection — a feed/linkout, never an embedded calendar.

Medium leverage Pass 0/3

Skeptics: useful passive-presence nicety, not a thesis-mover. Waypoint dates don't live where lives already are — the phone calendar — and subscribe-not-export earns daily lock-screen presence for the friend who never opens the app. Already backlog-specced; stays a feed/linkout (embeds off the table). Sequence after the Email Digest — prove the higher-leverage outbound channel first.

17

Universal Capture — one "add anything" front door

extends Platform · cross-cuts Itinerary, Documents, Money, Records, Ideation

A Platform-level single "add anything" affordance (the Trip Execution Add FAB generalized app-wide): photo / link / text / paste → classify → route to the right capability with a confirm step. A confirmation → Document, a restaurant link → Item-or-Saved-Reference, a thought → Memory, a receipt → Expense. The user never has to know Waypoint's taxonomy to feed it.

Medium leverage Pass 0/3

⚠ One skeptic flags it a shiny distraction. The friction is real (capture fragmented across ~7 surfaces), but the cure is a large horizontal classify-and-route engine bolted across six capabilities — the "over-build systems instead of using them" pattern. It hard-depends on the AI ADR and overlaps the Add FAB. If pursued, start as the narrowest single-input slice (paste-a-link routing), not an omnibox. Correctly sequenced last-ish.

Review of the revised map

A lighter re-review — the first council already fixed the structural defects. These are the sharpest modeling calls that hold under blue-sky pressure, plus the coherence/placement fixes that are still cheap edits.

What holds (validated across the lenses)

  • Availability as a distinct 4th mechanism. Member × date-range → yes/maybe/no, explicitly not a Vote — the map's sharpest modeling call, confirmed across 6 lenses. A "maybe" is a first-class third value with no Vote analog; aggregation is range-overlap, not score-sort. Folding it into goal_votes would have produced When2Meet-in-a-side-tab. Protect it against future "just reuse Vote" pressure.
  • Trip Execution kept at L1. Its zero-native-data is a feature, not a demotion signal — the pure live-lens surface where the most emotionally differentiated future work lands (capture fast-path, live nudges, in-the-moment memory, proactive replanning). Demoting it would have flattened the emotional spine into a CRUD app.
  • Logistics demoted to an Itinerary sub. Code-true: its code lives in lib/itinerary/; "ready by trip day" is a stage of the plan's outcome, not a peer outcome. (But see fixes — it stopped one notch too high.)
  • The "high bar before attaching to Item" guard. Matters more under expansion pressure — nearly every adjacent idea (Crew, Series, preference memory, participation) is tempting to bolt onto the over-loaded Item graph. The discipline pushes them toward new primitives instead.
  • Paid Moment decoupled from booked. Paid derived from a linked Expense, never a stored flag — the naive booked-flip toast mis-captures exactly the big group line items the feature exists for (deposit-then-balance) and trains reflexive dismissal.
  • Trip Memory's hard cap (ADR-0007). One photo + one thought per member per day, DB-enforced, member-only. The cap is the feature — it keeps Waypoint out of the photo-album NOT-zone and dodges the image-PII problem for the Public Archive by construction. Resist expanding it into a shared stream.
  • Onboarding as a sub of People & Membership (not Platform). Onboarding is a person becoming a participant, not the app loading. A zero-data sub is legitimate when its outcome is real and owner-assignable — same logic that keeps Trip Execution at L1.

Coherence & placement fixes (cheap edits)

  • Dissolve the Logistics sub one notch further. Only checklists/tasks (migration 0030) are owned; "readiness rollup" and "travel view" are pure projections. Move Booking under Itinerary directly; promote Item/Trip Readiness (booked·paid·documented) to a named cross-capability projection owned by none, fed by three; let Checklist/Task stand alone.
  • Fix the double-homed Checklist/Task. The Logistics Data line tags it 🟢-owned with no borrow-marker, so a reader gets two owners for one primitive. Mark it borrowed: 🔗 Checklist/Task (Itinerary-owned, ADR-0003) and state Logistics owns zero native data.
  • Differentiate Comment from Vote in the keystone section. Vote is 🟢 shipped/reused across three collections; Comment is 🔵 latent (a suggestions row, item-only — the polymorphic collection doesn't exist). Money and Documents both lean on Comment — planned-on-planned. Elevate "polymorphic comments collection" to a named blocking dependency; block expense-comments from shipping on the suggestions-row shape. Write the inverse-of-ADR-0004/0009 ADR (why Comment unifies where Vote stays split).
  • Promote Availability into the keystone-primitives section as the 4th input mechanism with its borrowers named (When-to-go, optional-Tuesday-hike, who's-around-for-dinner, the Travel view is an Availability projection nobody connected). Annotate its true blocker: gated on a dateless-trip lifecycle state (start_date/end_date must become nullable-until-resolved) — distinct from the other Ideation 🔵 objects, which are not schema-blocked.
  • Add Memory to Trip Execution's Add workflow. ADR-0007 says a Memory is "created during, consumed after," and charter #26 is mid-day capture — yet the live lens has no authoring door. Add Memory to the Add-workflow list; note authoring is dual-sited (in-the-moment + Closeout). Keep the collection Records-owned; only the surface is borrowed — same pattern as Glance.
  • Assign Saved Reference an owner. The map declares it "not a capability" but leaves it owned by nobody, violating its own ownership rule. Recommend Integrations & Syncing (literally "the manual twin of enrichment"), with Documents as the alternative. Pick one.
  • Rename Records & Archive to lead with the feeling. A filing-cabinet name carries the entire Remember pillar (the most emotional quarter of the lifecycle). Either rename (Memory & Record / Keepsake & Archive) or reorder the outcome to lead with the keepsake and demote portability to last; promote Trip Memory in the outcome line even while it stays a 🔴 sub.
  • State the Trip-Execution-kept / Logistics-demoted rule explicitly. Council rec #7 challenged both on identical grounds; the split reads as preference unless the discriminator is named: in-the-moment/offline is a temporally-distinct stance with its own surfaces; "ready by trip day" is continuous with planning. One sentence in the disposition.
  • Add an emotional-register clause to the NOT-list. The list guards logistical exclusions only — not the emotional versions of the same temptations (countdown hype → push; shared moments → feed/reactions; recap → AI authorship). One line: hype without push, shared moments without a feed or reactions, recap without AI authorship or auto-notify. Mark clones' roster-carry as a deliberate gap, not done.

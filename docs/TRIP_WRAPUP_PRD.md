# PRD — Trip Wrap-up State + Closeout & Publish Completion

> Owner: Scott Vanden Warsen
> Created: 2026-06-12 (from the #116 audit; charter decision D5; findings WP-A-001, WP-A-007, WP-A-019, WP-B-017, WP-B-018, WP-B-019, WP-B-020, and the archive-publish slice of WP-A-002)
> Status: Approved (modules + test scope confirmed 2026-06-12)
> Glossary: [[Closeout]], [[Public Archive]], [[Trip Member]], [[Trip Goal]], [[Parking Lot]], [[Expense]], [[Settlement]], [[Trip Mode]] in CONTEXT.md.
> Schema reality (verified in migrations 2026-06-12): `trips` has **no `status` field** — lifecycle is derived. The only persisted lifecycle flag is `archived` (bool, `0002_trips.js:25`). `finishCloseout` already sets `archived: true` (`closeout/+page.server.ts:111`) — the terminal transition exists. This PRD records the D6 fix to CONTEXT.md (status is derived, not a field) and a SPEC §4 role change.

## Problem Statement

A trip ends and the app goes quiet at the exact moment it should be loudest. The day after `end_date`, `isTripActive()` flips to false, the UI silently reverts from Trip Mode's 4 tabs to the 5-tab planning view, and the trip slowly slides into the "Past" section of the trips list with **zero call to action**. Nothing anywhere says "your trip ended — settle up, close it out, share the record." The three things a group wants to do after a trip are each buried: settle-up waits unprompted inside Money, the closeout wizard hides behind a More-tab card (owner/co-owner only — travelers and viewers get no post-trip surface at all), and the public archive link lives in Settings as a bare relative path in a code block. This is the entire "Remember" pillar's front door, and it doesn't exist (charter D5 pre-agreed this as a P1).

Worse, the parts that *do* exist are unsafe and dishonest:

- **The closeout wizard is lifecycle-blind.** It runs on a trip that hasn't started yet — an owner can mark items "done" and tap "Finish & Archive Trip" during planning. Archiving is a permanent one-way street: once `archived: true`, no route, action, or setting flips it back, Trip Mode can never activate, and nothing in any member's UI even indicates the trip is archived.
- **The publish gate lies.** The settings copy says the share link is "generated after the trip is archived," but the token is minted the instant the toggle is enabled; the "days after archiving" delay is actually measured from `end_date` and is skipped entirely once archived. An owner who enables the toggle mid-planning and never closes out has their full planned itinerary (titles, locations, addresses) published publicly under "What we considered" seven days after the trip ends — without ever consenting to publish.
- **The share moment has no handoff.** Finishing closeout (the natural "now share it" moment) redirects silently to the unchanged trip home with no link. The link itself is a relative path with no domain, no copy button, no share sheet. Before the publish date it 404s ("Archive not yet published") with no explanation — so a link texted to a non-technical relative on day one is a dead end on both sides of the publish window.
- **The record under-reports the trip.** The closeout wizard walks planned items day-by-day but never surfaces unplanned [[Parking Lot]] ideas or [[Trip Goal]]s, so every idea the group collected but didn't schedule is silently excluded from the public archive's "What we considered" section — even though SPEC and CONTEXT both claim the review step exists.

## Solution

Give the trip a derived **wrap-up state** that auto-renders the day after `end_date`, and complete the closeout-and-publish path so the record is honest, safe, and shareable.

- **Wrap-up home.** When a trip is past its end date but not yet archived, the trip Overview becomes a quiet, group-facing wrap-up screen with **three guiding cards in fixed order**: ① **Final expenses** — a CTA to add any last expenses, plus an optional, soft "Nothing more from me" check-in. It shows readiness as ambient info ("3 of 5 travelers ready") but **never blocks** anything. ② **Settle up** — the existing settlement surface, now invited instead of buried. ③ **Close out** — launches the closeout wizard.
- **Closeout for everyone who traveled.** The wizard opens to owner, co-owner, **and traveler** (viewers read-only) — a deliberate SPEC §4 change. It keeps the existing day-by-day done/considered walk and adds two review steps — **ideas review** (keep unplanned ideas for the record or drop them) and **goals review** (mark which [[Trip Goal]]s the group met) — then ends on an explicit **publish-timing** step: *Publish now* / *Wait N days* / *On [date]* / *Don't publish*. Finishing writes the publish choice, then sets `archived: true` — the deliberate, consented "closed" transition.
- **A record, not a dead end.** Once closed, the trip home becomes a read-only **Record view** (reusing the archive view builder) with a first-class **Share** affordance: a copyable **absolute** link, a plain-language status line ("live now" / "publishes on [date]"), and controls to change the date or disable sharing. Sharing is the *result* of closing out, surfaced where the group looks — not a buried toggle.
- **Closed isn't a trap.** An owner can **reopen** a closed trip — it drops back to its date-derived state (`wrap-up` if the dates are already past, `active` if somehow still in range), so an accidental/premature closeout or a late addition is fully recoverable. Reopening **pauses the public link** until the trip is closed out again, so a trip pulled back into editing is never left publicly published.
- **The public link is never a 404 before publish.** A pre-publish visitor sees a friendly branded "this trip's record will be visible on [date]" screen instead of a raw error page.

## User Stories

1. As a trip owner, I want the app to recognize the day my trip ended, so that I'm guided into wrapping it up instead of being dumped back into a planning UI with no prompt.
2. As a trip owner, I want a wrap-up home that lays out exactly what's left to do — final expenses, settle up, close out — in order, so that I don't have to remember where each task lives.
3. As a traveler, I want to see the wrap-up screen too, so that I can do my part (log my last expenses, settle up) without the owner chasing me.
4. As a viewer, I want to see the wrap-up state read-only, so that I understand the trip is over without being able to change anything.
5. As a trip owner, I want a clear CTA to add any final expenses, so that the money is complete before we settle.
6. As a traveler, I want an optional "Nothing more from me" check-in, so that I can signal I've logged everything without it being a hard gate.
7. As a trip owner, I want to see how many travelers have signaled they're done adding expenses ("3 of 5 ready"), so that I have a soft sense of readiness — but I never want it to block settling or closing out.
8. As a traveler who hasn't checked in, I want settling up and closing out to still work, so that one slow co-traveler never holds the group hostage.
9. As a trip owner, I want a prompt to settle up right on the wrap-up home, so that balances get squared while the trip is fresh.
10. As a trip owner, I want to launch the closeout wizard from the wrap-up home, so that the review is one tap from where I land.
11. As a trip owner, I want the closeout wizard to walk me day-by-day through planned items to mark each done or considered, so that the record reflects what actually happened (this exists today and is preserved).
12. As a traveler, I want to run the closeout wizard myself, so that I can contribute to the record even when the owner is busy.
13. As a viewer, I want closeout to be read-only for me, so that I can follow along without altering the group's record.
14. As a trip owner, I want a step that reviews our unplanned ideas — keep them for the record or drop them — so that "what we considered" reflects what the group actually weighed, not just what got scheduled.
15. As a trip owner, I want a step that reviews our trip goals — which did we meet? — so that the record captures what we set out to do, not only the itinerary.
16. As a trip owner, I want a final step to choose when the public record publishes — now, after N days, on a specific date, or never — so that publishing is a deliberate decision, not a side effect of a toggle I flipped weeks ago.
17. As a privacy-conscious owner, I want "Don't publish" to be a real, safe option, so that closing out the trip never forces my itinerary onto the public internet.
18. As a trip owner, I want finishing closeout to land me on a record view that hands me the share link, so that the "now share it" moment isn't a silent redirect to nowhere.
19. As a trip owner, I want the closeout wizard to refuse to run before the trip is over, so that I can't accidentally mark things done or archive a live trip during planning.
20. As a trip owner, I want a closed trip's home to show a read-only record of what we did, so that the trip has a satisfying final resting state instead of reverting to planning chrome.
21. As a trip owner, I want a copyable absolute share URL with a copy button, so that I can paste a working link into a group text without hand-assembling a domain.
22. As a trip owner, I want the share affordance to tell me in plain language whether the link is live now or publishes on a date, so that I know what my relatives will see when they tap it.
23. As a trip owner, I want to change the publish date or disable sharing after closeout, so that publishing decisions stay reversible even though the trip itself is closed.
24. As a non-technical relative who taps a shared link before the publish date, I want a friendly "this will be visible on [date]" screen, so that I don't hit a raw 404 that makes me think the app is broken.
25. As any member, I want a closed/archived trip to be visibly marked as such across its surfaces, so that nobody mistakes a finished trip for a live one.
26. As a group member, I want our unplanned ideas to appear in the public archive's "what we considered" section when we keep them, so that the keepsake reflects the breadth of what we discussed.
27. As a trip owner on a phone, I want the entire wrap-up and closeout flow to work one-handed at 375px, so that I can wrap up the trip from the airport gate on the way home.
28. As a trip owner whose trip ended in a non-UTC timezone, I want the wrap-up state to flip at the correct local moment, so that the app doesn't think my trip is still active (or already over) by several hours.
29. As a trip owner who closed out by mistake — or who needs to add something after closing — I want to reopen the trip, so that "closed" isn't a one-way trap and the trip returns to its normal editable state.
30. As a privacy-conscious owner, I want reopening a trip to pause its public link until I close out again, so that a trip I've pulled back into editing isn't still published to the world.

## Implementation Decisions

- **Trip lifecycle is derived, not stored.** A new pure module `trip-lifecycle` exposes `getTripLifecycle(trip, now)` returning a 4-state union: `'planning'` (today < start_date) / `'active'` (start_date ≤ today ≤ end_date, the current `isTripActive` truth) / `'wrap-up'` (today > end_date and not archived) / `'closed'` (archived — takes precedence over the date comparison, so an already-archived trip is always `closed`). All comparisons happen in the **trip-local timezone** (mirroring `tripToday`/`tripTz`), inheriting the #167 fix that `isTripActive` currently lacks (it compares against UTC `new Date()`). After this lands, `isTripActive(trip, now)` collapses to `getTripLifecycle(trip, now) === 'active'`. This is the load-bearing module — it gates every routing decision below.
- **The trip Overview is a lifecycle router.** The trip home branches on `getTripLifecycle`: `wrap-up` → wrap-up home (the 3 cards), `closed` → record view, otherwise the normal planning Overview. **Navigation is unchanged** — the 5 planning tabs stay; the wrap-up and record states are home-screen compositions, not new tab sets or modes.
- **Expense readiness is a soft, ambient check-in — never a gate.** New small module `expense-readiness`: `readinessSummary(members)` → `{ ready, total }`. New schema field `expenses_ready_at` (nullable date) on `trip_members` stores each member's check-in; a thin server action toggles the caller's own flag. The summary is displayed as info on the final-expenses card. No code path anywhere conditions settling, closeout, or publishing on readiness.
- **Closeout wizard gains two review steps and a publish step, and relaxes its role gate.** Step order: existing day-by-day done/considered → **ideas review** (each unplanned [[Parking Lot]] idea: keep-for-record [`considered`] or drop, with a bulk "keep all" — the long-specced end-of-phase review that was never built, WP-B-020) → **goals review** (mark which [[Trip Goal]]s were met) → **publish-timing** (Publish now / Wait N days / On [date] / Don't publish). On finish: write the resolved `archive_publish_at` (or clear it / disable sharing for "Don't publish"), then set `archived: true`. The wizard's loader gate relaxes from owner/co-owner to **owner/co-owner/traveler**; viewers are redirected (read-only follow-along is via the record view, not the wizard). This is a deliberate **SPEC §4 change** the PRD records (current §4 grants "use closeout wizard" to owner + co-owner only).
- **Closeout is gated to the wrap-up/closed lifecycle.** The wizard route gets a lifecycle guard mirroring the home router: it runs only when the trip is past its end date (`wrap-up`) or already `closed` (re-review). This kills the premature-entry half of WP-A-007 and the "archive a live trip during planning" half of WP-B-018 — archiving becomes reachable only deliberately, after the trip is actually over.
- **Archive visibility becomes explicit, replacing the leaky derived gate.** New pure module `archive-visibility`: `isArchiveVisible(trip, now)` and `publishStatus(trip, now)` → a small status union (`'live'` / `'scheduled'` (+ date) / `'unpublished'`). New schema field `archive_publish_at` (nullable date) on `trips` is the single source of truth for *when* the public route opens. The public `/archive/[token]` loader gates on `isArchiveVisible` instead of the current `end_date + archive_publish_after_days` math, and before the publish date it renders a friendly branded "visible on [date]" screen instead of `error(404, 'Archive not yet published')`. The legacy `archive_publish_after_days` field is left in place (append-only migration; it becomes the default seed for `archive_publish_at` when an owner picks "Wait N days", and stays for import/export/clone compatibility) but is no longer the gate. This resolves the consent breach in WP-B-019 (no publish without an explicit `archive_publish_at` the owner chose) and the pre-publish-404 slice of WP-A-002.
- **Record view reuses the archive builder.** The `closed`-state home renders read-only done-items via the existing `buildArchiveView` (same sanitization), plus the **Share** affordance: copyable **absolute** URL (origin + `/archive/[token]`), plain-language publish status from `publishStatus`, and controls to change `archive_publish_at` or disable sharing. Share is presented as the *result* of closing out, not a card in the wrap-up list.
- **Schema migration is append-only** (next migration `0047`): `trips.archive_publish_at` (date, nullable) and `trip_members.expenses_ready_at` (date, nullable). No deletions, no field renames — consistent with the project's never-delete migration rule.
- **Server actions are progressive-enhancement form actions**, not client fetch: the readiness toggle, the publish-timing write inside `finishCloseout`, and the share change/disable controls. Existing `finishCloseout` is extended to accept the publish choice (it currently takes no form body).
- **Documentation is corrected as part of this work.** CONTEXT.md's Trip glossary entry is updated to state the lifecycle is **derived** (4 states: planning / active / wrap-up / closed; only `archived` persists) rather than "status (planning/active/closed)" — the D6 live-doc fix. SPEC §4's permission table is updated to grant closeout to travelers. CONTEXT.md's Closeout and Public Archive entries get forward annotations referencing this PRD's ideas/goals review and publish-timing.
- **Closed is reversible — a guarded owner reopen.** A `reopenTrip` server action (owner/co-owner only, behind a confirm) sets `archived: false`; the trip then re-derives its lifecycle from dates via `getTripLifecycle` — there is no special "reopened" state, `wrap-up` or `active` simply falls back out of the dates. To stop a reopened, back-in-editing trip from staying publicly exposed, reopen also **pauses publishing** by clearing `archive_publish_at` (the public route's `isArchiveVisible` then returns false until the next closeout re-sets it). Together with the lifecycle gate and the explicit, default-safe publish step, this fully closes WP-B-018 — both the *accidental/premature archive* and its *irreversibility*. Reopen surfaces as a low-emphasis control on the record view.

## Testing Decisions

- Good tests assert **external behavior** — `(trip, now)` in, lifecycle/visibility/summary out — never internal structure. The three new pure modules are the high-value targets precisely because they're I/O-free and load-bearing.
- **Vitest, dense — the 3 pure modules:**
  - `trip-lifecycle`: all four states and their boundaries (day before start, first day, last day, day after end), the `archived`-takes-precedence rule (and the **reopen flip back** — `archived: false` on a past-end trip re-derives to `wrap-up`, not a special state), and the **non-UTC timezone boundary** (a trip ending in a negative-offset zone must not flip to wrap-up while it's still the final evening locally — the exact class of bug #167 found in `isTripActive`). Prior art: `activation.test.ts` (the existing `isTripActive` date-boundary suite).
  - `archive-visibility`: `isArchiveVisible` / `publishStatus` across unpublished (no `archive_publish_at`), scheduled-future, scheduled-today, live, and "don't publish"/disabled cases — the unpublished case also covers the reopen-pause (cleared `archive_publish_at` → not visible). Prior art: `archive-view.test.ts`.
  - `expense-readiness`: `readinessSummary` over zero/partial/all-ready rosters, ignoring removed/tombstone members. Prior art: `archive-view.test.ts`.
- **Playwright, one critical path:** trip ended → wrap-up home renders the 3 cards → launch closeout → walk to the publish step → choose **Publish now** → finish → record view shows a live, copyable absolute link. Prior art: `trip-mode-views.spec.ts`. Run `pnpm test:e2e` after the DOM changes; visual-verify the wrap-up home and record view at 375px.
- **Not separately unit-tested:** the wizard's new ideas/goals review steps and the readiness/share/publish form actions — these are UI + thin CRUD wrappers over the tested pure cores, covered by the E2E path above (consistent with "don't test trivial CRUD"). The confirmed test scope is the 3 pure modules + the 1 E2E, not the wizard steps in isolation.

## Out of Scope

- **The global branded error page** (`+error.svelte` / `handleError`) — that's the broader WP-A-002 fix, already filed as **#171**. This PRD only replaces the *archive pre-publish* dead end with a friendly screen.
- **Memory / "Note Before Bed" review in closeout** — Trip Memory is shelved (separate TRIP_MEMORY_PRD); no memory-review step here.
- **Cron/scheduled publishing.** Publishing stays on-demand (gate evaluated when `/archive/[token]` is hit), matching the current architecture — `archive_publish_at` just makes the gate honest.
- **Notifying members when the record publishes**, and any per-member publish consent beyond the owner's choice.
- **Changing settle-up or expense mechanics** — the wrap-up home only *surfaces* the existing settlement flow; it doesn't alter how settlements compute.
- **Multi-trip / trips-list bucketing fixes** (e.g. the "On trip · Now" mis-bucketing of archived trips noted in WP-B-018) beyond marking a closed trip as closed on its own surfaces — list-level bucketing can ride a follow-up.

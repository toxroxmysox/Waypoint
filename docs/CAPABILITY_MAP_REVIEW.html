Waypoint Capability Map — Council Review

An adversarial review of the grilled capability model · the home for a group trip: plan, execute, remember · solo to 12+.

Method. The grilled map (docs/CAPABILITY_MAP.md) was put through a 9-lens council (DDD / bounded-context, status-quo, data architect, adversarial, charter-fit, competitive, completeness, modeling-rules, provenance) and then independently red-teamed by 3 skeptics — 14 agents total — each verifying load-bearing claims against the live PocketBase schema, ADRs, and SPEC_BACKLOG.md. Every recommendation carries a skeptic consensus: CONFIRMED (≥2 of 3 call it real), CONTESTED (1), REJECTED (0). Schema claims below were re-verified in this worktree.  Date: 2026-06-21.

2Council findings

The signal that survived 14 agents, grouped by theme. The dominant pattern: the map asserts present-tense ownership of collections and fields the database does not have — several rationalized from ADRs the grilling walk authored hours earlier. "ADR accepted ≠ shipped" is the spine of the review.

Overlooked & misstated — as-built vs as-intended

The Data facet asserts ownership the schema doesn't back. Re-verified here: no money_unit collection or migration exists; confirmation_codes still lives on items (0006_items.js:39) while 0032_documents.js stores files only; booked_by/paid_by/cost_actual_usd sit dead in the Item schema. ADR-0016 is dated 2026-06-21 — the map claimed ownership from an ADR with zero migration behind it.

A reader trusting the Data facet models Documents/Money as code-owner and finds the data in Itinerary. The single most pervasive defect — touches 3 of 9 capabilities.

DDD · status-quo · data architect
High

Group Input "owns Vote + Comment" overclaims. Three deliberately unmerged vote collections exist (votes 0024, goal_votes 0042, suggestion_votes 0049 — ADR-0004/0009 forbid a polymorphic table). So Group Input owns a pattern (voting.ts), not one aggregate. Comment is not a collection at all — it's a suggestions.target_type='comment' row, item-only. The map's own Frontier admits both, contradicting its headline.

Read literally, "owns the Vote primitive" licenses the exact table merge two ADRs reject. (Value model is Love/Like/Flexible/Dislike, not "Pass".)

DDD · modeling-rules
High

Status-quo bias — granularity inherited from folders, not outcomes

The 9-box spine maps ~1:1 onto src/lib/ folders. Folder layout — not outcome analysis — plausibly set the granularity. Two nodes fail the map's own admission test (own data or earn a distinct outcome): Trip Execution owns zero native data (SPEC.md models it as "Trip Mode", UI state) and Logistics' only owned data is the Checklist/Task primitive — everything else is a view over borrowed data, which is a reporting layer, not a bounded context. The map applies the catch-all test to Logistics and Records but exempts Trip Execution.

Demoting both lets the same items/expenses count toward two L1s instead of inflating the L1 count.

DDD · adversarial · provenance
Medium

"Supersedes CONTEXT.md" is asserted but never mapped. lib/collaboration/ alone spans three of the map's capabilities (voting → Group Input, member-avatar → Membership, NotificationBell → Platform). The map instructs the reader to reconcile against a mapping that isn't written down — unactionable until an old-context → new-capability + lib/-split table exists.

provenance
High

External capabilities worth stealing — & the validated moat

The Candidate Scenario / decision board is genuinely ahead of the field. Doodle and When2Meet poll one axis; Trello and Pinterest don't structure a decision. Bundling several proposals into one weighable option is differentiating scaffolding — the Ideation fixes should plug into it, not replace it. Likewise Money correctly scopes to retire Splitwise (per-expense split + debt simplification + settlements + Paid Moment deriving "paid" from a linked Expense) — the strongest competitive front on the map.

Validated by the council, not changed. Keystone owns-vs-borrows model affirmed as the map's strongest DDD move.

competitive
Keep

Two committed connectors are missing from the canonical model. SPEC_BACKLOG.md commits Weather (Open-Meteo, no key — feeds packing readiness + Now-tab forecast) and an outbound Email Digest (Resend weekly "what changed"). The digest is verbatim "the realistic engagement lever for non-technical friends who won't open the PWA" — the only outbound re-engagement channel with push off-table, mapping straight onto the charter's core bar (the non-technical friend actually uses it).

completeness · charter-fit
Medium

Modeling gaps — unowned outcomes the charter flags

"When" cannot ride the Vote primitive — availability ≠ preference. A member can Love a week and be unavailable that week; they're orthogonal axes. The map justifies the Where/When/Why split by distinct outcomes yet assigns all three the same borrowed mechanism — an internal contradiction. An implementer reading it builds Vote-on-date-ranges and the group keeps a When2Meet tab open: a V1 charter failure. Needs a fourth mechanism — an Availability mark (member × date-range → yes/maybe/no overlap).

modeling-rules · charter-fit
High

Three real seams have no home in the model. (a) Post-join onboarding / "first five minutes" — charter D2, explicitly a likely-P1 gap — sits between Membership and Group Input. (b) Pre-trip soft-commit ("who's even in" before firm dates), a consequence of the verified dates-required hard-reject at trip creation. (c) The phase→phase idea hand-off (parked ideas stranded when a phase ends), an Itinerary/Parking-Lot edge. These are notes, not new L1s — building RSVP/onboarding as standalone capabilities is the scope-creep trap to avoid.

charter-fit · adversarial
Medium

Money Unit (ADR-0015) introduces a second people-grouping with a leave-able lifecycle that overlaps Membership's Member+Role. Without a stated cross-aggregate rule, "leave-able unit" + "removable member" yields orphaned or double-counted pooled debt — specifically the unstated case is a Member tombstoned mid-trip (ADR-0008) with balances. Cheap to state now (Money Unit references Member 🔗, never a second member store; resolves on tombstone). The map is silent; the heavier "promote Money Unit to keystone tier" idea is correctly rejected.

data architect · DDD
Medium
